"What exactly is a branded title vehicle and why are they trending in popularity?"
The explosion in popularity around buying branded title cars is simply due to education.
Car buyers are more savvy than they once were about branded titles and the extraordinary value and safety they provide. How are car buyers suddenly getting smarter about car buying? It could be the growing media coverage celebrating branded title cars or it might be the thousands upon thousands of people now driving branded title cars who are enthusiastically talking about how much they love their AutoSource branded title car.
A branded title vehicle is any vehicle which has experienced an insurance incident.
There are some levels of insurance claims that result in the title of a car being changed to represent the insurance incident. Let's say you get rear-ended in a parking lot and your car sustains significant damage. Even if you have the car professionally repaired and the car is as good as new, the insurance company can still have the title of your car changed from "Clean" to "Salvage". In other words, the title has been branded.
This "brand" has nothing to do with the current condition of the vehicle. Regardless of how great the car runs and looks after having the damage removed and returned to new, the title will be branded forever. That is why smart car buyers can purchase great cars at significantly lower prices. Branded title cars sell for lower prices even though they are exactly like their clean title counterparts. This gives wise car buyers a tremendous cost savings.
This value proposition is why branded title cars have become so popular and are getting harder to find.

    At AutoSource, we LOVE Level 1 and Level 2 Flood Cars!
    Our Certified Buyers use a very strict guide when acquiring vehicles. It is called The 4 Level Flood Standard. They classify each flood vehicle as either a level 1, level 2, level 3 or level 4. Here is a description of those four levels:

    • Level 1: Water has been near the vehicle and in some cases water has risen to mid-tire level, but has NOT entered the interior of the vehicle.
    • Level 2: Water has risen high enough to enter the bottoms of the doors, resulting in water engaging on the floorboards of the vehicle.
    • Level 3: Water has entered the vehicle and reached the bottom of the passenger seats.
    • Level 4: Water has entered the passenger cabin and has risen to the dashboard or above.
    NOTE: AutoSource Certified Buyers only seek out Level 1 and Level 2 flood vehicles. This is not true for others who sell branded title vehicles.
